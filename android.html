Android Apps

Creating Android Apps

I taught myself Java and set the challenge of creating a useful, fully functional Android app. The app below is the result, It took all of my free time for months on end. Making the app work is 12,000 lines of Java, thousands of lines of XML, a multi-table SQLite database and hundreds of images. The size of the task wasn't apparent to me when I first started but I was determined to finish it. In the end it sold less than 100 copies due to the over-saturation of the Android marketplace so I have recently made it free to download and many more people are now using it. I didn't expect it to earn me a lot of money since it was a project to help me learn (and learn I did!) and I'm happy with the result. The design deliberately goes against the "flat design" ethos that many apps go for since I wanted to provide an alternative to what others were doing.

The below paragraphs and screenshots are from it's listing on the app store.

Hwk.

Hwk. works around you, is fast, simple to use and extremely powerful. Keep on top of your assignments, homework, coursework, timetabled lessons, exams and session notes with little effort. Created by a lecturer and student who knows how study works.
Now completely free, no adds, no in app purchases. Enjoy!

Features:

Homework Diary
With just a few taps you can add a new homework, task or coursework assignment into the planner. Don't hang around in class typing details into a form that forces you to fill in every detail. Just set the name and due date and you're good to go then come back to it in your own time to set the optional details.

  • Sortable, fully functioning to-do list for each assignment
  • Choose customised subject from a drop down menu
  • Choose to be reminded about the assignment (see "Homework Reminder" below)
  • Check off when it's completed and enter the grade
  • Change any of the details at any time

The to-do list is designed so you can break down any assignment into manageable steps. Simply enter the next steps and check them off when done; never lose track of complicated tasks again!

Homework Reminder
Ever forgotten about a task or left work at home? Hwk. will remind you with a notification if you have any homework due in the next day, whether completed or unfinished. You decide the time to be reminded and which assignments to be told about. This can be switched off in the settings if you prefer.

See Upcoming Assignments
Assignments are sorted by due date in categories of Overdue, Today, Tomorrow, Next Week, After Next Week and Completed. See what's coming up and plan your work around your due dates. Overdue assignments are highlighted in red and completed assignments in green, see at a glance what is to be done in an order that makes sense.

Grade Tracking
Pressing the sort button groups assignments by subject. Use this to keep track of your progress, see what grades you've had back and how you're doing in each subject. Really useful for module-based courses where every grade counts.

Flexible Timetable
Turn your device landscape to see the traditional timetable layout, portrait to see the simple list view! Designed from the ground up to be extremely flexible but easy to use. Have a session on the 2nd Tuesday of every month? What about a short module running for just a few weeks in the middle of the year? Is your timetable different every other week? Want to include one-off sessions, tutorials, exams or presentation dates? All of this is as easy and as intuitive as setting up a standard weekly timetable of uniform sessions.

Timetable features include:

  • Sessions can start and end on any minute of the hour
  • Can be set to show any combination of days of the week
  • Will automatically re-size to include every session scheduled in a week
  • Sessions can be set to the following frequencies:
    • Weekly
    • Fortnightly
    • Monthly by date?
    • Monthly by nth day of the month (e.g. 2nd Friday of each Month)
    • One-off
  • Notes can be made for sessions
  • Edit individual sessions or all of a type at once
  • Set the colour highlight
  • Can be used for sessions and one-off events such as exams and events
  • Sessions are easy to set up; start and end date for a session is entered once for all sessions (can still be set to different for different sessions)
  • Length of a session and frequency is saved from one the last time it was set avoiding repetitive data entry.
  • Once the first session is in, to add a new one simply set a subject, time and day of the week. All other details are optional.

Subject Set up
Creating a subject is easy, in the "Subjects" section click on the plus icon, name it and choose one of over 70 unique subject icons.

Other Android Apps

I've made quite a few bits of apps, test projects, toys and games. Here are some of the more complete ones:

Beer Rater:
I made this app for a friend's wedding, they wanted to keep track of all of the quest beers they tried over the year leading up to their wedding so they could order a keg or two of their favorite for the big day. The app worked nicely and the beer went down well!

Bee Man:
A simple 2d platform game with destructible blocks and (somewhat) dynamic lighting. Made using LibGDX. Sadly never finished and so not available on the Play store.

Buttons:
An interesting little puzzle game with overly-complex rules! Essentially you placed coloured buttons on the grid and used them to fend off bad buttons that would break the board as they spread.
